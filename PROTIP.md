You can create separate .txt files for each of these categories on your SD card.

1. Aviation (Airband) - Best with your BNC/Telescopic Antenna
If you are near Subang or KLIA, these are the most active "Clear Voice" signals.

* Subang Tower: 118.200 MHz

* Subang Info (ATIS): 127.600 MHz

* KLIA Tower: 118.500 MHz / 118.800 MHz

* Lumpur Director: 125.100 MHz

* Lumpur Approach: 132.800 MHz

2. Amateur Radio (Ham) - Best with RH770
Ham operators in Malaysia are very active on these repeaters.

* 9M2RKK (Bukit Lanjan, KL): 147.980 MHz (Input: -0.6 MHz)

* 9M4RBC (Shah Alam): 147.750 MHz (Input: -0.6 MHz)

* 9M2RUK (Gunung Ulu Kali/Genting): 147.900 MHz (Covers almost all of Selangor)

* MARTS UHF Repeater (KL): 439.600 MHz

3. License-Free / Walkie-Talkies (PMR446)
Commonly used by security guards in malls, construction sites, and hikers.

* Analog Channels: 446.00625 MHz to 446.09375 MHz (8 standard channels)

* Digital (DMR): 446.103125 MHz to 446.196875 MHz

4. Shortwave (HF) - Best with your Donut Antenna
Since you are in Southeast Asia, you can easily catch these powerful international stations at night:

* BBC World Service: 11.965 MHz / 15.310 MHz

* Radio Free Asia: 9.350 MHz / 11.945 MHz

* China Radio International: 13.630 MHz (Very strong in Malaysia)

How to add these to your Portapack
Connect your SD card to your PC.

Navigate to the /FREQMAN/ folder.

* Create a new file called Malaysia_Scanner.txt.

* Enter the frequencies in this format: 118.200;Subang Tower;

* Save and insert the card back into your Portapack.
